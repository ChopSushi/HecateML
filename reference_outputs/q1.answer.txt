## Facts
- The discount window is a monetary policy instrument used by the Federal Reserve to provide short-term liquidity to eligible depository institutions. [1] [SOURCE-CHECKED]
- The facility allows banks to borrow funds from the central bank to manage temporary liquidity shortages resulting from either internal or external disruptions. [1] [SOURCE-CHECKED]
- Borrowing through the discount window is typically structured on a short-term basis. [1] [SOURCE-CHECKED]

## Answer
The discount window is a Federal Reserve facility that provides short-term liquidity to eligible depository institutions, allowing them to manage temporary cash flow shortages caused by internal or external disruptions [1]. 

The researcher's interpretation—that the discount window functions as a "lender of last resort" backstop—is supported by broader economic definitions. A "lender of last resort" is defined as a central bank that provides liquidity to financial institutions when they cannot obtain it through normal interbank markets, which aligns with the stated function of the discount window as a stabilizer during times of market stress [1][2].
